Friday 2-26: progress reports, march and the week ahead

2/26/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope all the kids enjoyed the snow day yesterday. It was a nice surprise, especially considering it's the first week back from winter break. Speaking of which, hearing from all the kids, it sounds like everyone had an awesome winter break as well. A lot of skiers in the area.
     Anyway, next week is March, which means the start of March is Reading Month. This year's theme is "Tech"nically, I love to Read and focuses on the use of technology in shaping the love/enjoyment of reading. There are quite a few fun activities, contests and dress-up/down days planned. It'll be a great month.
     Also next week, progress reports, which will go home in red folders on Friday. Please have them signed and returned to school as soon as possible.
     The rest of the week will look as follows:

     Sixth Grade: We're finishing up our unit on the United States and Canada. Notes for the study guide will be posted on the website under the "Unit II" section of sixth grade. Look for it the notes at the top of the page. We'll review Monday and the test will be Tuesday. From there, we're moving onto Latin America and the ancient civilizations of the Aztecs, Mayans and Incas.
     Seventh Grade: We have about two weeks left in our Middle East unit before moving onto Europe. We're currently discussing the Ottoman Empire and the expansion of the Islamic faith in the region. This'll lead us to the Ottoman Empire's decline just before World War I and the events after the Great War that helped shape the Middle East into the region we know it today.
     Eighth Grade: We're about five chapters into The Giver. Much of the discussion with the novel has focused on the characteristics -- laws, behaviors, living conditions -- of utopian/dystopian societies within the first few chapters, as well as the figurative language and the style and tone in which Lois Lowry begins her novel.
​
     Before I finish, I just want to congratulate the 7/8 girls on making the CYO basketball playoffs. They play Our Lady of the Lakes at 12:30 Saturday at St. Thecla. Best of luck to the girls.  
     I hope you all have a great weekend. 
     Mr. Hakim

Friday 2-5: Auction, Catholic Schools Week and week ahead

2/5/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     From the looks and sounds of things, Catholic Schools Week was a huge success. From Sunday's Open House to tomorrow's Auction and everything in between, the kids and staff had a blast. Thank you to everyone who had a hand in it, and a special thank you to all the roses and letters from Teacher Appreciation Day. Your children are the reason I love coming to work everyday, and to know they're having fun and enjoying learning in my class makes it all the more special.
     But nothing topped the smiles and excitement from Thursday's Special Persons' Day. Seeing the kids' eyes light up when their special person walked into the gym, or the smiles on their faces just being with that person, it's priceless. You and your children are what make St. Paul what it is, and thank you so much for doing so and for everything you do.
     We're a week away from Winter Break, and as crazy as it sounds, we're only a few months from the end of the year. That said, there's still a lot of work to do and it starts this week. Here's the breakdown of the upcoming week...
     Sixth Grade: We're learning about the 10 Commandments in Religion in preparation for Stump the Priest with Msgr. Halfpenny either Monday or Tuesday. Should be a fun afternoon, and if we stump him enough, the kids get an ice cream. So, hopefully Msgr. is off his game a little that day. In social studies, we're finishing up our compare/contrast infographs on the U.S. and Canada. We worked on comparing the neighboring countries according to health care, education, military, political parties, government, economics, geography and others. From there, likely after break, we'll move south to Central, Latin and South Americas and the ancient civilizations of the region.
​     Seventh Grade: We're still in the Middle East, discussing the development of religion and of civilization in the region. The class is currently working on a storybook regarding the origins and beliefs of the three major world religions that originated from the Middle East -- Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Afterward, we'll move quickly through the region's history on into the Arab Springs and the present-day culture and happenings.
     Eighth Grade: In literature, we started reading The Giver this week, and the class was asked to read an additional dystopian novel along with it. The intent being, we'll be able to compare and contrast the two novels according to authors' style and purpose (what are they warning us against, if anything?), the differences in governmental control and societal (dis)obedience, and the main protagonists -- the obstacles he encounters in trying to "free" the society from its oppression. It's a way to challenge them and to further develop their analytical/critical thinking minds by expanding the thought process into a deeper understanding of multiple texts.
     Finally, good luck to Beth Reilly and the 5th/6th grade Forensics team at the competition tomorrow morning at St. Lawrence. Having listened to some of the performances, I know St. Paul will have a strong showing tomorrow.
     Have a great weekend and enjoy the Super Bowl!
​     Mr. Hakim
