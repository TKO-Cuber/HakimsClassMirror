Friday 4-29: more dates and the week ahead

4/29/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     It's May, and it's shaping up to be an extremely busy month. In addition to the dates from last week, here are a few more to take note of...
  • Wednesday, May 4, at 1:30 p.m. -- ACES Day: It's a statewide program designed to motivate students to exercise.
  • Friday, May 6, after Mass -- May Crowning
  • Saturday, May 7, at 9 a.m. -- St. Paul Annual Fun Run/Walk
  • Wednesday, May 11 -- Bike Inspection Day
  • Thursday, May 12 -- Annual Picnic Lunch
  • Tuesday, May 24 -- Sixth Grade Field Trip to Toledo Zoo (See Permission Slip)
     Please let me know if you have any questions related to any dates posted this week or last. If I don't know the answer I can at least point you to the right direction or find out for you. 
     Additionally, there's a Family Satisfaction Survey that you'll receive next week. Yours and your children's feedback matter, so please take the time to fill out the survey. 
     That's all going on outside the classroom. Here's what to expect in class next week...

     Sixth Grade: The classes have discussed the Inca and how they adapted to their surroundings (in the Andes) in terms of weather and vegetation and food. They created relief maps out of Play Dough and contrasted where and how we get our food versus how the Inca get theirs. We're sticking with the Inca for another week, focusing more on how they lived and the various cultural advancements they made that still impact us today.
     Seventh Grade: I mentioned last week that we'd get into our unit on Russia. But before that, we're going to use our European Mini Market from last week as a stepping stone into a brief discussion of economics. For the next two weeks, we'll discuss scarcity, opportunity costs, supply and demand, specialization of labor and other topics related to economics and relate them to our Market and how we could actually start a business based on products from the Market.
     Eighth Grade: For the rest of the year, both classes are going to plan, design, write and publish an anthology of flash fiction, short stories and/or poetry. We'll publish it through the online distributor, Smashwords, and will have a launch party toward the end of the month. My hope is they get the real-world experience of marketing themselves, planning events, layout and design, website design, publishing and authorship. I'm also hoping they have a lot of fun with it.
     For that to happen, I need to make sure it's OK with all of you. I'll introduce the project to both classes Monday and will send home a parent permission slip as well. I'll also post all the project information on my website, in case you're curious about it. As well, I'll try to maintain weekly progress updates.
​  
     Have a great weekend!
     Mr. Hakim

Friday 4-22: important dates and the week ahead

4/22/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     It was good seeing some of you at the Spring Concert yesterday. The kids were incredible, and I was amazed at the talent on display with the solos and instrumentals. They all showed such poise, and it was remarkable to see it, especially from those who are otherwise so reserved/shy during the day.
     Another great thing was the 7th grade European Market Day. Thank you to all the parents who helped your sons/daughters make the food. I have to imagine some of you were told last minute, so it was probably a mad rush to get it done after the concert last night. It's all greatly appreciated and the kids had a lot of fun enjoying the tastes of Europe and learning about the different countries represented in the process. 
     Speaking of process, we're in the process of solidifying some important end-of-the-year dates. As noted in a previous update, the last day of school is now June 10. It's moved up a few days, giving us an earlier/longer summer break. Additionally, here are some dates for upcoming events/activities for the junior high...
  • 8th grade final exams are scheduled for the weeks of May 16 and 23. Finals Weeks opens with the literature final, May 16. Check the calendar for the remaining classes.
  • 8th grade Confirmation is May 20-21, Cedar Point trip is June 1, Park Day is June 3, and graduation is June 6.
  • 6th and 7th grade Park Day is June 7 at Pier Park.
  • There's also a field trip in the works for he 6th grade, possibly to the Toledo Zoo. It's tentatively scheduled for May 24, with more details to come soon.
  • TAP Basketball Finals and students v. teachers games are Tuesday, April 26. Teachers have added more talent this year, so we'll definitely be ready for the game. 
Please check back for any further updates to the schedule.
​     Here's a breakdown of next week's plans...

     Sixth Grade: We had a brief introduction into the ancient civilizations of Latin America this week and will continue next week learning about each civilization individually. We're starting with the Inca, focusing on how they used their environment/surroundings to thrive and survive as they did. As well, we've started developing an analytical reading comprehension skill based on the idea of Talking to the Text. It's a form of annotation and reading actively rather than passively. 
     In conjunction with the unit, we're planning for a debate at the end in which each group of students (three groups total) will debate the topic of Who is the greatest civilization?
     Seventh Grade: As mentioned above, the seventh graders concluded their European Market Day this week. We had representations from Italy, Spain, UK, Poland, Belgium, Lichtenstein, France, Switzerland, Ukraine, Czech Republic, Sweden, Greece, Ireland and others. Most made food, but some made crafts related to their country. It also had a business aspect to it as the students decided how much to charge for their product, the different strategies necessary to sell their products and the benefits of making money off their product.
     We'll begin next week with a debriefing on the unit, before getting into our unit on Russia. 
     Eighth Grade: We're working on our comparison/contrast essays for The Giver and another dystopian novel. Essays are due Friday, April 29. Students have to generate a thesis in relation to both novels and must use evidence from both novels to support their thesis/argument. The purpose is for them to learn to narrow their focus when writing and to write in an analytical and critical way as opposed to simply summarizing.    
​     
     Have a great weekend!
     Mr. Hakim

Parents,

     Have a great weekend and enjoy the break in the weather!

Friday 4-8: Accreditation, Summer and the week ahead

4/8/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope everyone enjoyed their vacations, whether traveling or staying at home, and had a nice Easter. We're nearing the home stretch, heading into the fourth quarter on Monday and with only two months left of school and few breaks between. Something important to note relating to breaks. Summer break just got a little longer. Instead of getting out June 14 as previously scheduled, the last day of school is now Friday, June 10. More information about it should be in the newsletter e-mailed out today.
     Another important announcement -- on Monday and Tuesday the Accreditation Team is visiting school, a "quality assurance" process that occurs every seven years. Being accredited is important and represents the school's continued commitment to standards of academic excellence, strengthening faith and building relationships. The team of associate superintendents, principals and teachers will sift through binders full of documented examples of this commitment. As well, they'll speak with a few students, teachers and others about the school. Parents, too, I'm sure, just in case someone stops you in the hallway to talk.
     Additionally on Tuesday, safety patrol will attend the Tigers game against the Pittsburgh Pirates, with lunch at Hockeytown beforehand. We also have Upland Hills Farm coming in Wednesday. Should be a fun and interesting time for all.
     Here's what to expect in class next week...
​
     Sixth Grade: The classes just finished making board games representing Latin America and its physical geography. We'll get into physical features' effects on the climate and vegetation of the region and will have a quiz on Wednesday or Thursday, before moving into the history of the region. Namely, the Inca, Maya and Aztec civilizations. 
     Seventh Grade: We started our unit on Europe this week, which encompasses a mini-market economy. Everyone chose a European country to research -- geography, history, culture, language, economy, etc. -- and a product associated with that country (Greek Mythology trading cards for Greece or crepes for France). With their research, they'll write a 4-6 page paper about their country. They'll also develop their product and conduct a cost-analysis of it for a market economy day at the end of the month.
     Eighth Grade: We read the most important chapters in The Giver this week and will move along rather quickly in the next week. Things are about to pick up steam in the novel, and I'd like to sustain that momentum all the way through, so there'll be quite a bit of reading done for homework next week, as well as in class with discussions and varying activities. We'll also begin focusing on the end-of-novel paper comparing and contrasting The Giver with another dystopian novel of students' choosing.

     I hope everyone has a great weekend!
     Mr. Hakim     .
