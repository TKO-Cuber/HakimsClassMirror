friday 12-4: a look into coming weeks

12/4/2015

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope you all enjoyed Thanksgiving holiday and break. Crazy to think that in two weeks we're already at Christmas vacation. With that comes progress reports, which will be sent home Friday, Dec. 18, the last day before break.
     In the weeks leading up to the 18, there's quite a bit going on at school, especially this coming week. On Monday we have reconciliation; Tuesday is an all-school Mass for the Feast of the Immaculate Conception; Thursday is the Young 5's to fourth grade Christmas Concert; and Friday is the school spelling bee. Times for everything are in the parent newsletter. 
     There's also the online auction for St. Paul's Auction 2016. The online portion started today and ends Sunday, Dec. 13. And basketball's regular season starts this weekend at St. Clare. Good luck to all teams!
     In addition to all the events, here's what we have upcoming in class.

     Sixth Grade: A few parents asked that I make mention of this, this being that if your child mentions needing his phone, iPad, Kindle, etc. for class, it's true. For the past month or so, we've been working on an extensive research project relating to the United States and Canada. We should finish the research part of it by Christmas break, and from there the groups will start putting together their presentations and challenges. So, if it's OK, they'll need their technology at least until break.
     This week we were introduced to culture through Cinderella. Students read the Perrault version (Disney's movies are based off this version), as well as the African version and Asian version. We dissected each story, working out the similarities and differences culturally among them. It'll lead into their research on the culture -- historically and currently -- of their U.S. region.
     Seventh Grade: We've officially completed our Sub-Saharan Africa unit and have moved on to North Africa and the Middle East.  During this unit, we'll focus on the water scarcity issue in the region, the birth of civilization and the three main world religions, and the current issues impacting us and the region.
     Eighth Grade: Classes have started reading The Pearl. Through whole-class discussions, we've really got into our analysis of the characters, themes, symbols, etc. They're currently working on a flowchart research assignment regarding how pearls are created. They'll be doing this as we continue to read through Chapter 2, during which John Steinbeck describes briefly the creation of a pearl just before Kino, the main protagonist, finds one in the ocean.

     As always, any questions, comments or concerns, please let me know. 

Have a great weekend!

Mr. Hakim

Friday 11-20: report cards, conferences and happy thanksgiving

11/20/2015

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I'm sure most of you already know by now that report cards went home today. Right away, you'll notice the different format/layout. Aside from that, the Archdiocese elected to return to the standard letter grade for conduct and has added effort to each class as well. Academically, everything remained the same as years past. For now. 
     Those with younger children are aware of the standards-based system. While every teacher at every grade evaluates students on benchmarks (what they should know at that point in the year), the elementary report card actually lists those standards and if the student met the standard requirements. Each report card will essentially tell an academic story of your child's progress/development. The Archdiocese hasn't completely developed the standards for junior high yet. It's still a work in progress, which is why the junior high report card hasn't adopted that format yet. Eventually, we'll all use the same standards-based report card system.
     As in past years, report card envelopes need to be signed and returned. Please do so as soon as possible.
     Other than that, next week is a short week. We have half days Monday and Tuesday due to conferences both days, and no school the rest of the week. 
     Still, here's what each class has been doing and will do in upcoming weeks.

     Sixth Grade: We're continuing our Amazing Race project. Classes have researched the various landforms, landmarks, bodies of water and currently the climates/weather of their regions. They'll finish with climate/weather Monday and Tuesday, and we'll get into history and culture after break.   
     Seventh Grade: I've pushed the test back until after break. We didn't get through everything I had hoped this week, so Monday and Tuesday will be dedicated to a final poster project and the test will likely be the Wednesday we get back. We'll review earlier in the week.
     Eighth Grade: We started reading The Pearl this week. It's a short 90-page novella by John Steinbeck, but there's a lot to it that we'll be working through and analyzing over the next few weeks. 

     I hope everyone has a happy and safe Thanksgiving. Go Buckeyes!

Mr. Hakim       

P.S. If anyone has any in-school news or school-related news (maybe a human interest story on a family or other community angle related to St. Paul), please let me know. We're currently working on the December issue of the Anchor News and welcome any story ideas for this issue or any upcoming issues. Thank you!

Friday 11-13: ARA, the dIA and report cards

11/13/2015

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     This week featured the start of ARA for the junior high. Feedback so far for the Alternative Recess Activities has been positive, and students have been enjoying the various electives available to them. Don't be surprised if some of the boys came home a little sore today after Boot Camp with Coach Barker. He's always good for a few push-ups.
     Just a reminder that next Friday is the junior high field trip to the DIA and report cards come home that day as well. Conferences start Monday, Nov. 23. My apologies for any inconveniences with conference times. If I haven't responded to your e-mail regarding a possible time slot, please e-mail me again, and I'll try to respond right away. Or if you haven't scheduled a time and would like to meet, please let me know and I'm happy to set up a time with you.
     With that said, here's a breakdown of the week ahead:
     Sixth Grade: Classes are hard at work researching the various landmarks, landforms and bodies of water in their respective U.S. regions. Starting Monday, we'll get into the human and cultural characteristics of the regions, followed by the history and development of the regions. After another week or two of research, each group will put together a series of challenges and clues to create a class version of THE AMAZING RACE.
     Seventh Grade: We're coming to an end in our Sub-Saharan Africa unit. Our focus this past week was on the devastating affects of decolonization and Apartheid. We'll now discuss Nelson Mandela's contribution to ending Apartheid and the current state of Sub-Saharan Africa. Expect a unit test either the Tuesday before Thanksgiving break or early the week after.
     Eighth Grade: Time to move on with the eighth grade. We're leaving the Civil War era and moving into Colonial times with John Steinbeck's The Pearl. It's written in the traditional style of moral parables, similar to those of the Bible. We'll start with a few introductory activities before starting the novella mid-week.

     I hope everyone enjoys their weekends. 

Thanks,

Mr. Hakim

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     Permission slips went home Thursday for the junior high's DIA trip Friday, November 20. There's a white sheet and a green sheet, the latter of which regards parent volunteers. If you're interested in volunteering, please send back the green form as well. Volunteers are extremely limited, so once we have an idea how many are interested, we can figure out numbers.
     Additionally, ARA (Alternative Recess Activities) begins next week. Today, your child received a daily account of activities they signed up for. I'm advising the Newspaper ARA, as well as helping out with several others. If you have any questions relating to Newspaper, please let me know. I'd be happy to answer them.
     The first quarter ended today as well, which means conferences are coming up in the next couple weeks. A Sign-up Genius e-mail was sent out to you a few days ago. Please sign up if you haven't already. If you're unable to attend, or if all the time slots are taken, let me know and we can set up a meeting another time. Report Cards will be sent home November 20. 
     Here's what we'll be working on in the next week:

     Sixth Grade: Social studies began its unit on the United States and Canada this week. What we'll be doing is creating our own version of THE AMAZING RACE based on the U.S. regions. Groups will research extensively information on their region's physical features, natural features and resources, culture, history, climate, etc. With that information, we'll further our understanding of how all the regions are connected and how it all impacts us in Michigan, and we'll use that information for future discussions on U.S. relations with Canada as we delve into the culture, history and features of Canada.

     Seventh Grade: In social studies we're currently working on the Scramble for Africa and European colonialism in the continent and its connection to Africa's current state of distress. During the next week, we'll focus on the various coups that occurred throughout the continent, attempts to overthrow European rule, as well as the lingering impacts of European rule. Events such as South African Apartheid and Nelson Mandela's promise of and fight for equality in the country.

     Eighth Grade: We've officially concluded the novel, Red Badge for Courage. Students are finishing up their projects this weekend in time for Monday's due date. This week we'll also watch the film and complete a comparison and contrast between the novel and the movie. Our next novel is John Steinbeck's The Pearl. 

     As always, please feel free to contact me with any questions, comments or concerns. I hope you all have a great weekend!

Mr. Hakim

0 Comments

 
Parents,
     Regarding my website, I've uploaded a few documents and essays for the sixth grade and eighth grade, respectively. These can be used toward extra credit for the class or for Classcraft.
     Sixth graders have a packet of activities for the Five Themes of Geography. They can do one or all the activities. Each one is worth 10 points on the game or two extra credit points in class. Extra credit for the Five Themes can be completed any time between now and our unit test date, which is Oct. 28. I mentioned last week that it was tentatively scheduled for the 21 but have decided to push it back a week. I'll have a study guide for them on Wednesday. Homework for this weekend -- Continue working on the Design Your Own Country projects, due Oct. 30.
     For the eighth grade, their extra credit consists of a few peer-reviewed essays and articles from Sports Illustrated and others relating to the novel, Red Badge of Courage. Students should read the essay or article and create a presentation on it, which both summarizes and offers a more in-depth look into it, and present it to the class. They can earn up to 500 points on Classcraft or 10 points in class. Homework for this weekend -- Work on the novel worksheets and plan the novel unit project, due Nov. 9. 
     Seventh graders are continuing the unit on Sub-Saharan Africa and are about to engage in a world simulation relating to European expansion into the continent. We've been discussing the desire of Europeans to colonize there, and now we'll get into the negative repercussions of it on African culture and economics. Homework for this weekend -- Read ch. 14 and complete the Section 1 review. Also analyze in greater detail the sections in the chapter about European expansion and the slave trade. Relate what's in your book to what we've talked about in class.
