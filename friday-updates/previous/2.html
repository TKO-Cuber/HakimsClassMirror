friday 3-11: reading month and the week ahead

3/11/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     We're two weeks into Reading Month and next week amps things up a bit. As a reminder, Monday is "Read a Button Day," during which everyone will wear a button they designed; Wednesday is "Dress Like Your Favorite Literary Character Day," and this year everyone dresses up, not just the teachers; Thursday is a $1 jeans day and favorite college team day; and on Friday we have author -- and former journalist for the San Francisco Chronicle and Dallas Morning News -- Kate Hannigan visiting to read and discuss her books, "Cupcake Cousins" and "The Detective's Assistant." Should be a great week for all.
     In addition, next week also begins Homeless Week, our St. Leo's Book Drive is ongoing for anyone who would like to donate new or gently used books, and Wednesday afternoon is an assembly for Jump Rope for Heart. More details are coming regarding JRH.
     Here's what's coming up in class next week as well...
​
     Sixth Grade: We've started our unit on Latin America, focusing for the first week on the political boundaries -- labeling the different countries and capitals in the area, differentiating the three regions, and understanding its location on a map. Next week, discussions will move into the physical features of the area and understanding the various landforms and waterways, landmarks and climate/vegetation.
     Seventh Grade: We're wrapping up the Ottoman Empire and moving onto the politics of the Middle East today. A unit test is scheduled for Friday. I'll have a study guide ready either Monday or Tuesday. And after break we'll start our unit on Europe.
     Eighth Grade: In Literature, next week will focus a lot on the understanding and importance of memories, as Jonas, once he's in his new position in "The Giver," is about to learn the truth behind his seemingly idyllic community It's the essence of Lois Lowry's story, one of the major reasons she wrote the story. We'll analyze the concept of memories through a series of studies, games, music videos, etc, as we read through the next few chapters in the novel.

     Best of luck to all Science Olympiad competitors this weekend.

     Have a great weekend!
     A.J.

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     Progress Reports went home today. The formatting is slightly different, but all the same information is there. One important note, though, is to please still sign and return the reports. There isn't a designated area to sign, but please still do.
     Next week is an especially eventful week here. On Tuesday, the seventh grade goes on its NET Retreat, while the sixth grade has its Seder Meal. We also have Reconciliation Monday morning and Stations of the Cross Thursday afternoon, and the eighth grade will attend Cross and the Light Friday morning. All the while, our March is Reading Month festivities kicked off this week and will continue up to Easter break. 
     Additionally, here's the breakdown for classes next week:
     Sixth Grade: The classes are working on physical and political maps relating to Latin America. It's an introduction to the unit and a way to get them acquainted with the region we're about to study. The maps will lead in to the study of geographical concepts in the region, or more specifically, how the people of the region interact with and shape the environment -- climate and vegetation.
     Seventh Grade: We're finishing up discussions on the Ottoman Empire and its influence on the Middle East region from the 1300s to World War I. On Tuesday we'll have a "coffeehouse" discussion relating to the fall of the Ottoman Empire and if/how it could've been prevented. Students researched an important figure of the time and will become that person in the discussions, using their research to form an opinion about what that person would say or do..  
     Eighth Grade: We've been reading and discussing "The Giver," working out the style and tone with which Lois Lowry writes, as well as the relating The Community and its rules/laws to our own lives. We'll continue reading chapters 6-8 next week and will focus on an analysis of the Ceremony of 12s.
     I hope everyone has a great weekend!

Friday 2-5: Auction, Catholic Schools Week and week ahead

2/5/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     From the looks and sounds of things, Catholic Schools Week was a huge success. From Sunday's Open House to tomorrow's Auction and everything in between, the kids and staff had a blast. Thank you to everyone who had a hand in it, and a special thank you to all the roses and letters from Teacher Appreciation Day. Your children are the reason I love coming to work everyday, and to know they're having fun and enjoying learning in my class makes it all the more special.
     But nothing topped the smiles and excitement from Thursday's Special Persons' Day. Seeing the kids' eyes light up when their special person walked into the gym, or the smiles on their faces just being with that person, it's priceless. You and your children are what make St. Paul what it is, and thank you so much for doing so and for everything you do.
     We're a week away from Winter Break, and as crazy as it sounds, we're only a few months from the end of the year. That said, there's still a lot of work to do and it starts this week. Here's the breakdown of the upcoming week...
     Sixth Grade: We're learning about the 10 Commandments in Religion in preparation for Stump the Priest with Msgr. Halfpenny either Monday or Tuesday. Should be a fun afternoon, and if we stump him enough, the kids get an ice cream. So, hopefully Msgr. is off his game a little that day. In social studies, we're finishing up our compare/contrast infographs on the U.S. and Canada. We worked on comparing the neighboring countries according to health care, education, military, political parties, government, economics, geography and others. From there, likely after break, we'll move south to Central, Latin and South Americas and the ancient civilizations of the region.
​     Seventh Grade: We're still in the Middle East, discussing the development of religion and of civilization in the region. The class is currently working on a storybook regarding the origins and beliefs of the three major world religions that originated from the Middle East -- Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Afterward, we'll move quickly through the region's history on into the Arab Springs and the present-day culture and happenings.
     Eighth Grade: In literature, we started reading The Giver this week, and the class was asked to read an additional dystopian novel along with it. The intent being, we'll be able to compare and contrast the two novels according to authors' style and purpose (what are they warning us against, if anything?), the differences in governmental control and societal (dis)obedience, and the main protagonists -- the obstacles he encounters in trying to "free" the society from its oppression. It's a way to challenge them and to further develop their analytical/critical thinking minds by expanding the thought process into a deeper understanding of multiple texts.
     Finally, good luck to Beth Reilly and the 5th/6th grade Forensics team at the competition tomorrow morning at St. Lawrence. Having listened to some of the performances, I know St. Paul will have a strong showing tomorrow.
     Have a great weekend and enjoy the Super Bowl!
​     Mr. Hakim

Friday 1-15: news, notes and the week ahead

1/15/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope all the kids enjoyed the extra day off today! It was a busy week in the junior high for social studies and literature. Sixth graders organized all their information from the past couple months into slideshows and Prezis and posters and presented their research to both classes. On the Tuesday we get back, they'll use the notes from each presentation to complete an AMAZING RACE-type challenge in the classroom.
     For literature, the eighth graders finished reading The Pearl this week and conducted a mock trial Thursday relating to the novel's main protagonist and if his actions were committed in self defense or murder. It was a fun week for me seeing it all unfold, and I hope the kids enjoyed it just as much.
     Sixth Grade: The fun will continue next week with the AMAZING RACE challenge Tuesday. It's the final piece to all the hard work and research both classes completed the past couple months. In concluding the project, we're officially done with phones, iPads, computers, etc. for the time being. Thank you for allowing the kids to bring them in each day -- and some multiple devices. It's greatly appreciated, and I hope it proved worthwhile for the kids.
     Seventh Grade: We're moving into discussions on the ancient civilizations, starting with Mesopotamia. During the past week, the class discussed what makes a civilization and how they developed in time. Now, in looking at Sumer and other Mesopotamian city-states, we'll talk about all the technological advancements and the developments of a writing system, government, religion, etc.
     Eighth Grade: With The Pearl done, we're moving onto The Giver and our unit on utopias and dystopias. Knowing that the majority of the grade has already read the novel, I like to bring new perspectives into it, giving them a new experience the second -- or third or fourth -- time around. Within the unit, we'll read additional short stories and listen to music relating to the idea of dystopia and utopia. We'll also draft our own ideas of utopia and dystopia. It's an interesting unit and really opens students' minds to better understand the world around them.  

     Have a great weekend!
          Mr. Hakim

Parents,

     I'd like to start by congratulating the Spelling Bee participants and winners. It takes a special talent to compete in Forensics, and St. Paul has some special people representing us tomorrow and in the upcoming 5th/6th grade competition. Best of luck to the Forensics team as well.
     A quick note before getting into next week, please check out St. Paul's online auction, if you haven't already done so. The auction closes Sunday at 10 p.m. Visit stpaulonthelake.com and click the Auction logo to enter the site and bid. 
     Moving onto next week. Here's a breakdown of what to expect in class for the week.

     Sixth Grade: Our AMAZING RACE project is nearing its completion. We're finishing up on the culture and history and natural resources pieces, and on Monday we'll start putting together our research into the group presentations. Our plan is to have the presentations completed by Friday. When we return from break, we'll give a quick review, the groups will put together challenge questions for the "race," and then they'll present their projects.
     We also have our Christmas Party Friday afternoon. A special Thank You to Mrs. Kesteloot and, Mrs. Borrego for putting it together. 
     Seventh Grade: We've started our North Africa and Middle East unit, having worked on the physical features and political maps of the region this past week. On Monday, we'll shift our focus slightly to the question of, How can the physical environment and climate affect how people live? We'll discuss the water scarcity issue in the region, as well as its heavy reliance economically on petroleum and natural gas.
     Eighth Grade: Classes are now midway through The Pearl. We're starting to see a change in the main character's beliefs and attitude. They have a one-page analysis due Wednesday regarding that behavioral change. Next week, we'll relate the novella to "The Parable of the Hidden Treasure and the Pearl" from Matthew 13:44-46. Many critics believe Steinbeck took literal meaning from the parable's message during the writing process of his novella. Based on the parable's meaning, the class will relate it to the novella and offer predictions of what's to come. 

     I hope everyone has a wonderful weekend! Good luck this weekend to the athletics teams, Forensics and any other programs, school-related or not.

Mr. Hakim      ​
