Friday 1-29: report cards, open house and auction 2016

1/29/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     Report cards went home today. Please review them, sign and return the envelope to school Monday, which also is the beginning of Catholic Schools Week for the kids. A CSW events schedule went out last week, detailing each day's activities and theme. As a quick refresher, Monday is red, white and blue jeans day in support of the Veterans. Participation is 1.00, and all proceeds will benefit the Veterans. Review the letter from last week for the rest of the week's plans. I'll also remind the class each day.
     While Monday starts CSW for the kids, the official kick-off is this Sunday, January 31, with the St. Paul Open House. It runs 11-1:30 and is a great event/opportunity for anyone interested in their children pursuing a Catholic education. Please tell anyone you know who may be interested to attend Sunday.
     CSW concludes the following Sunday with the Auction 2016, at the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, Another great event for the school, I'm looking forward to seeing a lot of you there.
     Through the excitement of CSW and the varying activities, we still do get to work. Here's the breakdown of next week's classes...
     Sixth Grade: We're closing the book on the U.S. and Canada unit this week. The classes are working on comparing and contrasting the two countries based on government, economics, geography, history, political parties, education and other such factors. We're answering a series of questions regarding each and creating an infographic chart of the comparisons.
     Seventh Grade: We have a little more time left talking about the major world religions of the Mesopotamian Middle East before moving on to present-day Middle East and the Arab Spring and other events affecting and influencing the region. This week, we're comparing and contrasting Judaism, Christianity and Islam, and will create a storybook 
     Eighth Grade: Anticipation has been building for us to starting reading The Giver. We've done a lot of work breaking down/analyzing the characteristics of a Utopia and of a dystopia. Using songs, music videos, short stories and other literature/media, we've really wrapped our heads around both ideas and engaged in some very interesting discussions. Now it's time to take what we've learned and apply it to the novel. It'll be an interesting read. 
     I hope you all have a great weekend! As always, any questions, comments or concerns, please e-mail me and I'll try to respond as soon as possible.
 
     Thanks,
     Mr. Hakim

Friday 1-22: announcements and week ahead

1/22/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I don't mean to sound like a broken record the past few weeks, but there are a few important school events upcoming, Open House and Auction 2016. Both are invaluable to the future of the school, so it's important we all get the word out and make them as successful as can be. Open House is Sunday, January 31, from 11-1:30, and Auction 2016 is Saturday, February 6, at the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club. RSVP for the auction by next Friday, January 29. Hope to see you all there!
     Now, here's what to expect for class next week...
     Sixth Grade: The AMAZING PROJECT project is over, but we're staying in North America for the time being, at least the next week or so. During that time we're taking what we learned from research during the project and comparing and contrasting that information with Canada. Discussions will focus on Health Care, Government, Economics, Military, Political Parties, Education, Geography, Culture and History. Also, we have a test Monday regarding presentations from the AMAZING RACE project. Students can use their notes and work in their groups to answer the questions.
     Seventh Grade: Still in Ancient Mesopotamian civilizations, we're discussing the birth of the three main religions this week. Namely, the differences and similarities among them. Students will then create a storybook relating class discussions and notes on the topic.  
     Eighth Grade: We're under way on our Giver unit, having discussed utopias and dystopias, author's style, tone and purpose. This week, we'll further analyze the concepts of utopias and dystopias through music, the Bible and short stories, before getting into the actual novel, which will likely be Friday or the following Monday.
     As always, please e-mail me with any questions, comments or concerns. I hope everyone has a great weekend!
​     Mr. Hakim

Friday 1-15: news, notes and the week ahead

1/15/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope all the kids enjoyed the extra day off today! It was a busy week in the junior high for social studies and literature. Sixth graders organized all their information from the past couple months into slideshows and Prezis and posters and presented their research to both classes. On the Tuesday we get back, they'll use the notes from each presentation to complete an AMAZING RACE-type challenge in the classroom.
     For literature, the eighth graders finished reading The Pearl this week and conducted a mock trial Thursday relating to the novel's main protagonist and if his actions were committed in self defense or murder. It was a fun week for me seeing it all unfold, and I hope the kids enjoyed it just as much.
     Sixth Grade: The fun will continue next week with the AMAZING RACE challenge Tuesday. It's the final piece to all the hard work and research both classes completed the past couple months. In concluding the project, we're officially done with phones, iPads, computers, etc. for the time being. Thank you for allowing the kids to bring them in each day -- and some multiple devices. It's greatly appreciated, and I hope it proved worthwhile for the kids.
     Seventh Grade: We're moving into discussions on the ancient civilizations, starting with Mesopotamia. During the past week, the class discussed what makes a civilization and how they developed in time. Now, in looking at Sumer and other Mesopotamian city-states, we'll talk about all the technological advancements and the developments of a writing system, government, religion, etc.
     Eighth Grade: With The Pearl done, we're moving onto The Giver and our unit on utopias and dystopias. Knowing that the majority of the grade has already read the novel, I like to bring new perspectives into it, giving them a new experience the second -- or third or fourth -- time around. Within the unit, we'll read additional short stories and listen to music relating to the idea of dystopia and utopia. We'll also draft our own ideas of utopia and dystopia. It's an interesting unit and really opens students' minds to better understand the world around them.  

     Have a great weekend!
          Mr. Hakim

P.S. Some additional notes...
  • Open House is Sunday, January 31, from 11-1:30. If you have anyone interested in their child(ren) attending St. Paul, please have them visit, or have them stop in for a school tour. ​
  • Auction 2016 is almost here as well. It's Saturday, February 6, at the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, and is a great community event with a lot of awesome raffle and auction items. Expect the themed basket raffle tickets flier to come home Tuesday. 
  • Catholic Schools Week runs the first week of February, culminating in the Auction. It'll be a fun week for the kids, who'll participate in numerous activities and service opportunities throughout the week.

Friday 1-8: The week ahead

1/8/2016

0 Comments

 
     Happy New Year! It feels like forever ago that I last wrote a Friday update. Listening to the kids, it sounded like everyone enjoyed their holiday breaks or vacations, which is great to hear. Before we get into the week ahead, a few notes: 
     We're in need of lunch volunteers and recess duty. Contact the office if you're interested and able to help out. 
     Please remember, there's no school for the junior high this Friday, January 15, and no school for everyone Monday, January 18.
     Open House, Catholic Schools Week and the Auction 2016 are fast approaching. Open House is Sunday, January 31, from 11-1:30. Please pass the information along to anyone you know who could use it, or who knows someone else who could use it. Catholic Schools Week is the week of February 1. More information will follow in the coming weeks as we get it. Auction 2016 is February 6, at the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club. It's a great event and a great way to support the school, as it's our biggest fundraising event of the year.
     Here's a breakdown of next week...
     Sixth Grade: Our AMAZING RACE projects are all but complete. Groups are putting the finishing touches on their presentations and will present them likely Tuesday and Wednesday of this week, with the actual race taking place Thursday. It'll be fun to see the results of all their hard work the past month or so. From there, we'll start with the relationship between the United States and Canada, then move on to South America and the ancient civilizations.
     Seventh Grade: The class just started Mesopotamia and the other Early River Valley Civilizations. We'll spend the next week talking about the birth of government, writing, and religion as well as major advancements in technology from this era.
     Eighth Grade: I hope everything went well today for the eighth graders who went to North or South to choose their classes. In class next week, we'll be finishing The Pearl and will begin our unit on dystopias/utopias, during which we'll read The Giver.
     As always, please contact me with any questions, concerns or comments. Thank you for all you do, and have a great weekend!

Mr. Hakim
