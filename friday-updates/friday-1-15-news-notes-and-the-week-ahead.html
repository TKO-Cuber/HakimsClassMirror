Friday 1-15: news, notes and the week ahead

1/15/2016

0 Comments

 
Parents,

     I hope all the kids enjoyed the extra day off today! It was a busy week in the junior high for social studies and literature. Sixth graders organized all their information from the past couple months into slideshows and Prezis and posters and presented their research to both classes. On the Tuesday we get back, they'll use the notes from each presentation to complete an AMAZING RACE-type challenge in the classroom.
     For literature, the eighth graders finished reading The Pearl this week and conducted a mock trial Thursday relating to the novel's main protagonist and if his actions were committed in self defense or murder. It was a fun week for me seeing it all unfold, and I hope the kids enjoyed it just as much.
     Sixth Grade: The fun will continue next week with the AMAZING RACE challenge Tuesday. It's the final piece to all the hard work and research both classes completed the past couple months. In concluding the project, we're officially done with phones, iPads, computers, etc. for the time being. Thank you for allowing the kids to bring them in each day -- and some multiple devices. It's greatly appreciated, and I hope it proved worthwhile for the kids.
     Seventh Grade: We're moving into discussions on the ancient civilizations, starting with Mesopotamia. During the past week, the class discussed what makes a civilization and how they developed in time. Now, in looking at Sumer and other Mesopotamian city-states, we'll talk about all the technological advancements and the developments of a writing system, government, religion, etc.
     Eighth Grade: With The Pearl done, we're moving onto The Giver and our unit on utopias and dystopias. Knowing that the majority of the grade has already read the novel, I like to bring new perspectives into it, giving them a new experience the second -- or third or fourth -- time around. Within the unit, we'll read additional short stories and listen to music relating to the idea of dystopia and utopia. We'll also draft our own ideas of utopia and dystopia. It's an interesting unit and really opens students' minds to better understand the world around them.  

     Have a great weekend!
          Mr. Hakim

P.S. Some additional notes...
  • Open House is Sunday, January 31, from 11-1:30. If you have anyone interested in their child(ren) attending St. Paul, please have them visit, or have them stop in for a school tour. ​
  • Auction 2016 is almost here as well. It's Saturday, February 6, at the Grosse Pointe Yacht Club, and is a great community event with a lot of awesome raffle and auction items. Expect the themed basket raffle tickets flier to come home Tuesday. 
  • Catholic Schools Week runs the first week of February, culminating in the Auction. It'll be a fun week for the kids, who'll participate in numerous activities and service opportunities throughout the week.
